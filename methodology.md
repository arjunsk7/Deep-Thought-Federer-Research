# Methodology Document — DeepThought Target Company Research

## Objective

The objective of this assignment was to identify manufacturing companies in Ahmedabad that fit DeepThought’s “Federer Company” profile — technically differentiated, growth-oriented manufacturers that are likely to value structured execution systems and internal capability building.

The focus was not on simply collecting company names, but on filtering for companies that matched DeepThought’s ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) using the provided scoring framework.

---

# City and Segment Selection

I selected:

* **City:** Ahmedabad
* **Primary Segments:**

  * Industrial Automation & Instrumentation
  * Specialty Manufacturing / Industrial Electronics
  * Specialty Chemicals (selectively)

Ahmedabad was chosen because it has a strong manufacturing ecosystem across chemicals, automation, industrial electronics, instrumentation, engineering systems, and process industries. It also contains a large number of mid-sized promoter-led industrial companies, which aligns well with DeepThought’s target profile.

---

# Research Process

My research process was iterative rather than linear. Initially, I explored broader manufacturing categories, but many companies turned out to be traders, system integrators, or generic engineering firms that did not fit the Federer profile.

Over time, I refined the search toward more technically differentiated manufacturers.

The process generally followed these steps:

1. Search for companies within a target segment
2. Check whether the company actually manufactures products
3. Verify Indian manufacturing operations
4. Identify technical depth and differentiation
5. Evaluate founder/leadership background
6. Look for growth signals and recent activity
7. Score the company using the Federer framework

# Filters Used

The main filters I applied were:

* Ahmedabad operational presence
* Manufacturing company (not only services)
* Employee size greater than ~100 wherever possible
* Technical or differentiated product portfolio
* Promoter-led or technically driven organization
* Active website and visible business activity
* Mid-sized company range preferred
* Industrial or specialty manufacturing orientation

I avoided:

* pure distributors/traders
* generic fabrication shops
* system integration-only firms
* electrical contractors
* large conglomerate subsidiaries
* inactive companies
* companies with no visible product differentiation

---

# Scoring Logic

I used the Federer scoring framework provided in the assignment.

Each company was evaluated across six criteria:

1. Manufacturing capability
2. India-based operations
3. Product differentiation
4. Technical decision-maker
5. Sector tailwinds
6. Growth signals

Each criterion was scored as:

* Weak
* Moderate
* Strong

The total Federer score was then calculated out of 100 based on the weightage provided in the assignment brief.

Special importance was given to:

* differentiated products
* industrial R&D
* Industry 4.0 positioning
* instrumentation/electronics capability
* technical founders/promoters
* export orientation
* automation and process-industry relevance

# Key Learnings

One major learning from this assignment was that many companies initially appear to fit the ICP but fail after deeper verification.

For example:

* many “automation companies” were actually only system integrators
* many “engineering companies” were fabrication shops
* several technically strong firms failed due to ownership structure or lack of manufacturing depth

Another important learning was the difference between:

* a generic industrial SME
  and
* a differentiated technical manufacturer

The strongest companies consistently showed:

* technical specialization
* proprietary products
* industrial R&D
* automation/electronics depth
* export orientation
* active growth signals

I also learned that finding true Federer-type companies requires iterative filtering and judgment rather than simple keyword searching.

---

# Conclusion

This research process helped build a more structured understanding of India’s mid-sized specialty manufacturing ecosystem, especially within Ahmedabad’s automation, instrumentation, industrial electronics, and specialty manufacturing sectors.

The final list was curated not only on industry relevance, but also on promoter mindset, differentiation, technical capability, and visible growth orientation — all of which are central to DeepThought’s target profile.
